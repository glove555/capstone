Amino Acids

Amino acids are any group of organic molecules that consist of a basic amino group (―NH 2 ), an acidic carboxyl group (―COOH), and an organic R group (or side chain) that is unique to each amino acid.


Since R-groups are unique - they can affect the pH/pKa of the chains and also result in a different isoelectric point (pI value). The isoelectric point can then be used to identify amino acid matches. Furthermore, amino acids can be grouped based on specific properties special to them (ie. +/- charge, polar/nonpolar, etc)

As amino acids are exposed to acids (protonated) or bases (deprotonated) they gain (protonated) or lose (deprotonated) Hydrogens resulting in differing structures. Amino acids are also one of few compounds that form zwitterions. In the case of amino acids this occurs when the NH3 group has not been deprotonated into the NH2 group and COOH has been deprotonated to form COO-. Titration curves will have areas of buffering depending on how many pKas there are. If there are 3 pKAs like in Glutamate - there will be three "humps" or curvy areas to the curve. If there are two pKa there will typically only be 2 humps.
